# Two-Week Work Plan
## SEMTM0045 Portfolio - Theme 2 Topic 1

This document outlines a two-week working plan for completing the portfolio on **Evaluation under Uncertainty and Limited Data**. The plan is designed to ensure that topic scoping, literature review, gap identification, methodology design, writing, and viva preparation are completed in a structured way.

---

## Overall goal

The overall goal of this two-week plan is to produce a complete portfolio that includes:
- a critical literature review,
- a clearly defined research gap,
- a justified proposed methodology,
- practical and ethical considerations,
- a non-technical summary,
- and supporting GitHub evidence showing the development process.

---

## Day 1
### Focus: Project setup and topic confirmation
- Confirm the scope of Topic 1 and finalise the working research question.
- Create and structure the GitHub repository.
- Set up key folders for planning, research evidence, report drafting, and final submission.
- Create initial working documents, including the paper matrix and reading notes.

### Expected output
- Repository structure completed
- Initial README file created
- Working research question confirmed

---

## Day 2
### Focus: Initial literature search
- Identify and collect the first set of relevant papers related to cross-validation, evaluation uncertainty, small sample instability, and class imbalance.
- Record core bibliographic details and first impressions.
- Begin the paper matrix.

### Expected output
- At least 8 to 10 relevant papers identified
- Initial paper matrix drafted
- First reading notes created

---

## Day 3
### Focus: Literature clustering and core paper selection
- Group the literature into major thematic clusters.
- Identify the most important foundation papers and supporting papers.
- Start distinguishing between background literature and key papers for close reading.

### Expected output
- Literature clusters defined
- Core papers selected
- Key paper summary document started

---

## Day 4
### Focus: Critical reading of core papers
- Read the most important methodological papers closely.
- Identify the main claims, assumptions, contributions, and limitations of each paper.
- Expand the key papers summary and literature notes.

### Expected output
- Core paper notes substantially expanded
- Stronger understanding of methodological foundations
- More specific ideas for the research gap

---

## Day 5
### Focus: Research gap identification
- Review patterns across the literature.
- Identify what is well covered and what remains insufficiently addressed.
- Draft a clear gap analysis for the portfolio.

### Expected output
- Gap analysis drafted
- Link between literature and research question strengthened

---

## Day 6
### Focus: Objectives and study direction
- Refine the research question if needed.
- Write a small set of focused and achievable objectives.
- Clarify the intended contribution of the portfolio.

### Expected output
- Objectives finalised
- Study direction made more precise
- Clearer transition from literature review to methodology

---

## Day 7
### Focus: Methodology design
- Draft the proposed methodology.
- Decide which evaluation protocols, model types, and metrics will be included in the study design.
- Write the first full methodology sketch.

### Expected output
- Methodology sketch completed
- Validation protocols and metrics justified

---

## Day 8
### Focus: Data, ethics, and practical considerations
- Consider the data conditions required for the proposed study.
- Identify practical risks, ethical issues, and reporting challenges.
- Draft notes on responsible evaluation and overconfident claims in limited-data settings.

### Expected output
- Practical considerations section outlined
- Ethical risks and mitigation ideas recorded

---

## Day 9
### Focus: Introduction and literature review drafting
- Draft the report introduction.
- Draft the main critical literature review section.
- Connect background, instability, imbalance, and uncertainty into a coherent argument.

### Expected output
- Introduction draft completed
- Literature review draft completed

---

## Day 10
### Focus: Gap and methodology drafting
- Draft the research gap and objectives section in report form.
- Draft the proposed methodology section in report form.
- Ensure direct alignment between the literature critique and the proposed study design.

### Expected output
- Gap/objectives section drafted
- Methodology section drafted

---

## Day 11
### Focus: Practical sections and non-technical summary
- Draft the data, ethics, and practical considerations section.
- Draft the non-technical summary.
- Ensure that the report can be understood both technically and practically.

### Expected output
- Practical considerations section drafted
- Non-technical summary drafted

---

## Day 12
### Focus: Full report integration
- Combine all report sections into a full draft.
- Check structure, coherence, and repetition.
- Reduce weak transitions and improve argument flow.

### Expected output
- Full first draft assembled
- Structural issues identified for revision

---

## Day 13
### Focus: Revision and formatting
- Revise the report for clarity, concision, and academic tone.
- Check references, figures, and page limit.
- Make sure the report fits the required structure.

### Expected output
- Near-final draft completed
- Formatting and citation checks completed

---

## Day 14
### Focus: Final submission preparation and viva preparation
- Export the final PDF.
- Update the GitHub repository so that the evidence trail is complete.
- Prepare short viva slides and a brief spoken overview of the portfolio.

### Expected output
- Final PDF ready
- GitHub repository updated
- Viva preparation materials completed

---

## Final note

This two-week plan is intentionally intensive. It prioritises continuous progress, evidence of development, and clear alignment between literature, research gap, and methodology. The goal is not only to complete the portfolio on time, but to demonstrate a structured research process throughout the repository.
